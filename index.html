          Conduit Intelligence Framework

          How Conduit identifies, scores, and prioritizes regional telecommunications opportunities.

          What is Conduit?

          Conduit is a regional telecommunications intelligence platform that aggregates development, infrastructure, utility, land, builder, county, and GIS activity into a unified operational view.

          Core Principle

          Conduit does not make decisions. It identifies and prioritizes areas for investigation by surfacing patterns across multiple intelligence signals.

          What is a Signal?

          A signal is any activity that may indicate future telecommunications demand, infrastructure investment, development growth, or network expansion.

          • Fiber Construction
          • Directional Bore Activity
          • Utility Expansion
          • Residential Development
          • Builder Activity
          • Large Parcel Activity

          What is an Opportunity?

          Opportunities are identified when multiple independent signals converge within the same geographic area.

          Opportunity Index

          The Opportunity Index is a weighted signal score. Higher values indicate stronger signal convergence and greater justification for investigation.

          It is not a 0-100 score, percentage, probability, or forecast.

          0-5Low Signal
          6-10Moderate Signal
          11-15High Signal
          16+Priority Opportunity

          Telecommunications Use Cases

          Conduit is designed to support regional telecom expansion strategy by turning scattered development, land, utility, and infrastructure activity into actionable market intelligence.

          • Growth Corridor Identification: identify areas where development and infrastructure activity are concentrating.
          • Competitive Activity Awareness: monitor utility and telecom-related activity that may indicate market movement.
          • Builder Engagement Opportunities: identify active builders and communities that may require broadband coordination.
          • Broadband Expansion Planning: support early review of future service areas and potential network growth.
          • Market Intelligence: consolidate county, land, builder, and infrastructure data into one regional view.
          • Infrastructure Monitoring: track locate and construction activity that may indicate utility or network investment.
